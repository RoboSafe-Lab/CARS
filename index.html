Learning Responsibility-Attributed Adversarial Scenarios
for Testing Autonomous Vehicles

Yizhuo Xiao  ·   Haotian Yan  ·   Ying Wang  ·   Zhongpan Zhu  ·   Yuxin Zhang  ·   Mustafa Suphi Erden  ·   Cheng Wang

Heriot-Watt University  ·  Tongji University  ·  University of Shanghai for Science and Technology

Abstract

Safety validation of autonomous driving systems requires adversarial scenarios that are not only severe but also attributable to the system under test. We present CARS (Context-Aware, Responsibility-attributed Scenario generation), a closed-loop framework that couples a context-aware adversary with integrated responsibility attribution. A Gaussian Mixture Diffusion Model (GMDM), pre-trained on naturalistic driving data and refined through reinforcement learning, generates behaviourally diverse adversarial trajectories. A learned selection module dynamically identifies the most threatening surrounding vehicle at every simulation step, so the adversary tracks the evolving scene rather than being fixed before simulation.

A careful and competent driver model (CCDM) drawn from UN Regulation No. 157 then evaluates whether each generated collision is attributable to the target AD system: the scenario is retained only when a competent driver could have avoided the collision.

On 408 collision scenarios generated from nuScenes urban traffic, 403 (98.8%) are attributable to the target, with 52.7% reaching the FSM Hard criticality tier. The same trained policy transfers zero-shot to two naturalistic drone datasets with contrasting scene geometries, retaining 94.5% attribution on AD4CHE highway traffic and 82.1% on the RounD roundabout.

Zero-shot Cross-dataset Transfer

The same frozen CARS policy, trained only on nuScenes urban traffic, is applied without any retraining to two naturalistic drone datasets with contrasting scene geometries. Both videos are rendered on the original drone photo backgrounds provided with each dataset.

AD4CHE highway traffic
Frozen nuScenes policy applied to a Chinese highway drone recording. The context-aware module continues to identify the most threatening surrounding vehicle, and 94.5% of the generated collisions on AD4CHE pass the CCDM responsibility check.
RounD roundabout
Frozen nuScenes policy applied to a four-arm roundabout drone recording at Neuweiler, Germany — a scene geometry absent from the training distribution. 82.1% of the generated collisions on RounD still pass the CCDM responsibility check.

Context-aware Adversary Selection

The adversary role dynamically transfers between agents mid-episode based on a learned classifier, maintaining a coherent threat across all three datasets without any retraining.

Responsibility Attribution

Each generated collision is evaluated under three CCDMs drawn from UN R157, so the attribution conclusion does not depend on a single driver model.

CARS is the only method that simultaneously achieves high responsibility attribution (98.8% FSM valid), adversary kinematics within production-vehicle bounds (a95=2.26 m/s², jerk95=15.64 m/s³), and balanced coverage across all three criticality tiers. Each baseline fails on a different axis: Bezier produces smooth but non-severe trajectories, STRIVE exceeds physical kinematic limits, and SafeSim collapses into degenerate Hard-only distributions.

Most unpreventable cases arise from encounter-geometry limits (short TTC, physical braking deficit) rather than CCDM-specific artefacts, confirming that the AD system under test should not be held at fault for them.
