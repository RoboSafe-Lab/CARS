Adversarial Scenario Generation for Autonomous Vehicles

Abstract

Existing closed-loop scenario generation methods for autonomous driving (AD) validation often struggle to jointly preserve kinematic realism, behavioral diversity, and meaningful safety criticality. To address this challenge, we propose CARS, a two-stage framework for adversarial scenario synthesis. CARS augments the diffusion policy with a Gaussian mixture output head to capture diverse driving intentions, and further refines the policy through reinforcement learning (PPO) to induce safety-critical interactions. To improve the consistency of threat construction, we introduce a context-aware adversarial-agent selection module with temporal confirmation for stable adversary assignment. To ensure evaluation relevance, we further employ a careful and competent driver model (CCDM), instantiated with a Fuzzy Safety Model (FSM, UN R157), to determine whether the generated collisions are the responsibility of the AD system under test. Extensive experiments on the nuScenes dataset demonstrate the effectiveness of CARS in stress-testing AD systems. CARS generates 408 collision scenarios, and CCDM verification confirms that 403 of them are the responsibility of the target AD system, where a competent driver could have avoided the collision yet the target failed to do so.
